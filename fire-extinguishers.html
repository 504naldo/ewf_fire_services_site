More Than a Tag

A tag on a fire extinguisher confirms it was inspected — but inspection quality matters. An extinguisher that is low on pressure, improperly mounted, blocked from access, or past its service life may fail when it's needed most.

NFPA 10 requires annual inspection, 6-year internal maintenance, and hydrostatic pressure testing at intervals that vary by extinguisher type. Non-compliant extinguishers can create liability for building owners and may not be recognized as adequate by the Authority Having Jurisdiction.

EWF provides clear documentation of each unit's condition, service history, and required follow-up — giving property managers and strata councils a complete record for AHJ inspections and insurance purposes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do fire extinguishers need to be inspected in BC?
Fire extinguishers require annual inspection under NFPA 10 and the BC Fire Code. A 6-year internal maintenance service is also required, along with hydrostatic pressure testing every 5 to 12 years depending on extinguisher type.
What happens during an annual fire extinguisher inspection?
EWF technicians check the extinguisher's pressure gauge, tamper seal, pull pin, mounting bracket, accessibility, physical condition, and tag. Any unit that fails inspection is flagged for recharge, maintenance, or replacement with a clear explanation of what's required.
Can EWF recharge and replace fire extinguishers on site?
Yes. EWF can recharge extinguishers following use or following the 6-year maintenance interval, replace units that are damaged or past service life, and provide updated compliance tags and documentation.
